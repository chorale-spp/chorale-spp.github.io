A consistent vocal warm-up protects your voice and prepares it to give its best. Here is a practical ten-minute routine you can use before every rehearsal or performance.

## The Routine (10 minutes)

### 1. Body & Breath (2 min)

Stand with feet hip-width apart. Roll your shoulders back and down. Stretch your neck slowly side to side. Take three slow low breaths (see our article on choral breathing). Yawn widely — this opens the throat naturally.

### 2. Lip Trills (2 min)

On a comfortable mid-range pitch, produce a sustained lip trill (*brrr*). Slide gently up and down in pitch. This warms the voice without strain and coordinates breath with phonation.

### 3. Humming (2 min)

Hum on *"mm"* through a comfortable range. Feel the resonance in your face — the bridge of the nose, the cheekbones. Humming is gentle on the vocal folds and encourages forward placement.

### 4. Vowel Sirens (2 min)

On *"wee-oh"* or *"ng-ah,"* slide smoothly from your lowest comfortable pitch to your highest and back down. Keep the slide connected — no breaks. This wakes up your full range without forcing it.

### 5. Scale Patterns (2 min)

Sing a simple 1-3-5-3-1 pattern on *"ma-me-mi-mo-mu"* starting in a low key and stepping up by half steps. Keep the tone light and open. Stop before you strain.

## A Note on Cold Voices

Never begin a rehearsal by singing at full volume. A voice that hasn't warmed up is like a muscle that hasn't stretched — it can be damaged by sudden exertion. *Give it ten minutes. It will give you everything in return.*
